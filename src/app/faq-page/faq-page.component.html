FAQs

Who are you?

I'm Alex Hinton, a computer scientist in my fourth year of study at New College of Florida. I am working on BTS-Dash with Dr. Tania Roy, Assistant Professor of Human Centered Computing at NCF.

Why did you make this?

The initial website was a final project for Dr. Tania Roy's Software Engineering class in the Spring 2021 semester at NCF. This website, which is a continuation of that project, is an exercise in bringing together information on one subject from across many different social media platforms. BTS, which has a social media following and subsequent online presence of nigh unheard of proportions, is an ideal topic for this.

Where did your Twitter data come from?

The tweets are being pulled directly from Twitter with their API. The official tweets are pulled using the Twitter API endpoint that pulls the tweets from a particular user from the last 7 days, and it pulls from the two official BTS Twitter accounts, @bts_bighit and @bts_twt. The tweets that are displayed in the feed on bts-dash are only the ten most recent of these posts. The trending tweets are pulled using the Twitter API endpoint that pulls the trending tweets from a specific hastag. We are pulling from #BTS on Twitter and displaying the ten most recent posts.

Where did your follower counters come from?

The follower counters are being pulled from livecounts.io and mixerno.space, using the embedded counters provided. They update, roughly, every 10 seconds.

Where did your Spotify data/track rankings come from?

We are pulling the track rankings for each country from Spotify's API. The rankings for each country are an internal calculation that Spotify does and isn't directly related to the site-wide popularity ranking of songs. Spotify doesn't release how it calculates what the most popular songs for each country are, so we're just trusting Spotify on this one.
